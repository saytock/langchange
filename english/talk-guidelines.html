①Preparation

Please make sure that you are using Google Chrome or Firefox.
Check audio and video of your laptop.
Learner decides the talk menu (exercise or freetalk). If you choose exercise, you should prepare some sentences for reading practice. (see detail at exercise tab)

②Attendance Checking

"Standby" button will show up 5 minutes before the talk starts. Click it and wait for your partner to be ready.
If you missed to click it by 10 minutes after starting time, you will lose 2 LC coins as a penalty.

③Start the Talk

When you and your partner have both already clicked the standby button, video window shows up and automatically start the call. Have a great time!
Introduce yourself to each other. Then, move to free talk or exercise.
Tutor should try to talk slowly so that the partner can understand. If your partner is a beginner, it's important to speak clearly and make your words concise.
Use the chat box freely below the video.

④-Step1 Self-introduction

Introduce yourself! Tell your name, and talk about your school, major, clubs, something you're into, etc. For begginers, self-introduction would be a great way to practice the second language. Do it many times with different partners. It will get easier and more comfortable to speak Japanese as you keep trying speaking.
If you're still nervous, see guidelines for self-introduction.

④-Step2 Free Talk

Talk freely! Examples for topics would be differences and similarities between cultures, something weired about your country.
Use nonverbal language like gestures. Don't fear to make mistakes. What's important is how much you use the language.
If you need more topic examples, see the tips below.

④-Step2 Exercise

Choose exercise if free talk seems difficult.
1 Prepare
Learner must prepare 3-5 short sentences in the other language before the talk. Don't use difficult and thick books. Just an easy basic text is enough to improve your conversation skills.
If you don't have any books for practicing, see Tips at the bottom.
2 Ask your partner to help with the exercise
Learner suggests his/her partner to do the exercise.
エクササイズがしたいです。(Exercise ga shitai desu.)
I want to do the exercise.
3 Read the sentences
Read the sentences and practice speaking. Ask your partner to correct your pronunciation during reading.
発音をなおしてください。(Hatsuon wo naoshite kudasai.)
Please correct my pronunciation.
4 Practice
After reading some sentences, try making your own sentences using words that you practice.
単語をいくつか変えてみましょう。(Tango wo ikutsuka kaete mimasho.)
Let me change some words in this sentence.
Replace some words by words that you know.
E.g. 昨日、私は学校にバスでいきました。(I went to school by bus yesterday.)　⇒　昨日、私はさくら公園に自転車でいきました。(I went to Sakura park by bicycle yesterday.)
5 Make them your own
After talking, remember the sentences that you learned and try using them in everyday conversations. The more sentences you remember, the more fluently you can talk.
